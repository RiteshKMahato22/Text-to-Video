How To: Go on Vacation (Checklist)

Rebecca 23 Feb 2024
05 Comments 33K Views

1. Plan Ahead: Before you even start packing your sunscreen and flip-flops, take a moment to consider the impact of your absence on your work commitments. Here's a checklist to get you started: Add your vacation dates to the company calendar to keep everyone in the loop. Have a chat with your manager to discuss any projects or tasks that might be affected by your absence. If you're part of any rotational duties or have specific responsibilities like being a Major Incident Manager, make arrangements for someone to cover for you.

2. Communicate Effectively: Communication is key, especially when it comes to managing expectations. Make sure you: Set up an out-of-office email response and update your Slack status to let colleagues know you're away. Consider creating a detailed out-of-office page outlining your absence duration and any crucial information. This not only helps your team but also ensures you can truly disconnect and unwind.

3. Delegate and Cover Your Bases: Think beyond your immediate tasks and consider all the ways your absence might impact your team. Take proactive steps to: Delegate responsibilities and tasks to ensure nothing falls through the cracks. Set up overrides for any rotational duties or critical roles you're responsible for. Check if there are any other processes or team dependencies that require your attention and address them beforehand.

4. Extend Your Reach: For longer vacations spanning more than a couple of weeks, consider going the extra mile: Create a personalized out-of-office page for a seamless transition in your absence. Customize your Slack and email messages to reflect your availability and expected return date. By following these steps, you can bid farewell to that nagging feeling of uncertainty and confidently step into vacation mode. Remember, a well-deserved break not only rejuvenates your spirit but also enhances your productivity and overall well-being. So, go ahead, pack your bags, and embrace the bliss of a worry-free vacation. Your future self will thank you for it!

If you are a Major Incident Manager, Be sure you have someone to cover your rotation, and schedule an override.Set an override on the Trello MIM OpsGenie schedule.

Are you on a disturbed rotation? - Be sure you have someone to take your place, and schedule an override. See Trello Mobile Rotations Overview for all the Mobile rotations and Trello OnCall - OpsGenie for all Trello schedules. This may include your team DoS rotation, release train, all hands, etc, so be thorough.

Handover Meetings: Schedule handover meetings with key team members to brief them on the status of ongoing projects and any pending tasks that need attention during your absence. This ensures a smooth transition and minimizes disruptions in your absence.

Documentation and Instructions: Prepare detailed documentation outlining the procedures and protocols for handling your responsibilities in your absence. This may include step-by-step guides, FAQs, and troubleshooting tips to assist your colleagues in resolving any issues that may arise.

Plan for Re-Entry: Anticipate the workload awaiting your return by setting aside time in your schedule to catch up on emails, meetings, and pending tasks. Prioritize your tasks based on urgency and importance to ease the transition back into work mode after your vacation.
